Weightlifting

History

I started weightlifting seriously in the year 2017. My motivations were somewhat different and disorganized compared to most. My whole life, like many young men, I had wanted a six pack. I was chubby beforehand and chose to simply go on a diet. Over the course of 3 months, I went from 165 to 125 pounds (I am 5'6"). In losing these forty pounds, I had achieved my goals, but now, I had to find a way to mantain the rebound weight gain such that I could still have the physique I desired. After a month or so of doing cardio post-weight loss, I decided I hate cardio and would rather weight train. I have been lifting since this moment, over four years, and have managed to maintain the physique I once desired and am overall quite happy with myself.

Goals

At this point, I am hovering around 130 pounds, plus or minus two. My strength is relatively high, though, somewhat below my personal records, with a max bench press of 250lb, squat of 365lb, overhead press of 150lb and deadlift of 405lb. I am at a bit of a crossroads here. Through my goal of desiring an incredibly low body fat percentage (6%) I am also rather small for a bodybuilder. I am hoping to be able to maintain a low body fat like this with a higher muscle mass. Ideally, I would say that I would maintain this level of leanness at a weight of around 140lb.

Routine
